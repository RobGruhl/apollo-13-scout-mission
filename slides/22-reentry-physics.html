Background Information

Re-Entry Corridor Physics

The Narrow Window

Re-entering Earth's atmosphere from the Moon requires hitting a corridor just 2.15 degrees wide - like threading a needle from 200,000 miles away.

Too Steep (-7.4° or steeper)

Result: Burn up

Too much friction, too much heat. Heat shield fails at 5,000°F+. Crew dies.

Just Right (-6.5° target)

Result: Safe landing

Gradual descent, heat shield works, parachutes deploy. Crew survives.

Too Shallow (-5.25° or shallower)

Result: Skip off atmosphere

Bounce back into space like a stone on water. Lost in orbit. Crew dies.

Apollo 13 hit -6.49° - nearly perfect!
