Background Information

What Caused the Explosion?

The Root Cause

The explosion wasn't caused by anything the crew did wrong. It was a series of small mistakes and oversights that accumulated over years.

The Chain of Events
  • 1965: Oxygen tank #2 manufactured with 28-volt heaters
  • 1968: Spacecraft upgraded to 65-volt system, but tank heaters not upgraded
  • 1970 (pre-launch): Tank dropped 2 inches during removal, caused internal damage
  • Detanking test: Used heaters to boil off oxygen, exceeded safe temperature
  • Result: Teflon insulation on wires damaged, creating short circuit risk
The Explosion Moment

When Jack Swigert flipped the switch to stir the tanks, damaged wires sparked, igniting the Teflon insulation. The resulting fire caused pressure to build rapidly until the tank exploded, blowing a 13-foot panel off the Service Module.

The Lesson: Small problems that seem minor can cascade into catastrophic failures. Attention to detail matters in spaceflight.
