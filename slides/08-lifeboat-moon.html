Lifeboat & Moon Flyby

April 13-14, 1970 | Aquarius Becomes Home

Moving to the LM

At GET 56:00:00, with the Command Module dying, the crew made the critical move to the Lunar Module "Aquarius."

The Squeeze

  • Cramped: ~160 cubic feet (like a walk-in closet)
  • Designed for standing room, not living quarters
  • Three grown men in bulky space suits
  • Moving one arm disturbs the other two
  • No privacy, no comfort, no sleep possible

Swinging Around the Moon

Day 4 - April 14, 1970

Apollo 13 reached its closest approach to the Moon: ~158 miles above the surface. Through the LM window, the crew watched the lunar surface rush below.

  • Pericynthion: Closest point to Moon. Should have been landing at Fra Mauro right now. Instead, just passing by for survival.
  • Communication Blackout: Spacecraft passes behind Moon. No radio contact with Earth. Minutes of silence. Families hold their breath.
  • Signal Returns: They're still alive!

The PC+2 Burn

At GET 79:27:40, the crew performed a critical speed-up burn:

  • LM descent engine fired for 4.5-5 minutes
  • Added ~900 ft/s velocity
  • Shortened trip by 9-10 hours (crucial for conserving resources)
  • Targeted Pacific recovery zone near USS Iwo Jima
  • Engine performed flawlessly - trajectory set for Earth

  • Minimal heaters (too cold to sleep)
  • Minimal lights (stumbling in dark)

  • Moving disturbs others
  • Exhaustion building
