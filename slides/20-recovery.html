Chapter 6: Home

Recovery Operations

April 17, 1970 | GET 142:54:41+ | Pacific Ocean

GET 142:54:41 - SPLASHDOWN!

🌊 IMPACT!

Location: South Pacific Ocean, near Samoa

Coordinates: 21.5°S, 165.4°W

Distance from target: 0.6 miles (incredibly accurate!)

Speed at impact: 22 mph

ALL THREE ASTRONAUTS ALIVE.

The Impact

What the Crew Felt:

Odyssey slammed into the Pacific Ocean at 22 mph—like hitting a wall in a car crash. But they were ready.

  • 💥 WHAM! - Violent impact, crew jolted forward against harnesses
  • 🌊 Capsule tipped over, went underwater briefly
  • 💨 Inflation bags deployed automatically (uprighting system)
  • ⬆️ Capsule rocked upright to Stable 1 position (nose up)
  • 🌊 Bobbing in ocean swells
  • ✅ All systems nominal—no water leaks

First Words After Splashdown:

JIM LOVELL: "We're on the Iwo, Houston. Apollo 13 is on the Iwo Jima."

(Translation: We're safe. We made it.)

Physical Condition After Impact:

  • 😌 Relieved: Finally home, finally safe
  • 💪 Exhausted: 6 days of hell, barely functioning
  • 🤒 Fred Haise: Still feverish (103°F), severely dehydrated
  • 💧 All three: Dehydrated, lost 12-14 pounds each
  • 😴 Sleep-deprived: ~5-8 hours total sleep over 6 days
  • 🥶 Hypothermic: Body temperatures still low from days of cold

But they were ALIVE. And that was all that mattered.

USS Iwo Jima - The Recovery Ship

Ship Specifications:

  • Type: Amphibious assault ship (LPH-2)
  • Crew: 667 sailors + Marines
  • Helicopters: 20+ on board for recovery operations
  • Position: 3.6 miles from splashdown point (perfect positioning)
  • Recovery team: Navy UDT (Underwater Demolition Team) swimmers

The Ship's Role:

The Iwo Jima had been on station for days, waiting. The crew watched Apollo 13's descent on radar, ready to respond immediately.

  • 🚁 Helicopters launched as soon as parachutes deployed
  • 🏊 Swimmers in the water within 2 minutes of splashdown
  • 🚁 Recovery helicopter hovering over capsule within 3 minutes
  • ⚓ Ship moving to pickup position

Recovery Timeline

GET 142:54:41 - Splashdown

Odyssey hits the water. Recovery operations begin immediately.

GET 142:56:00 - Swimmers Arrive (1.3 minutes)

Navy UDT swimmers jump from helicopters, swim to capsule:

  • Attach flotation collar around capsule
  • Secure sea anchor to prevent drift
  • Inflate additional stability bags
  • Check for hull damage or leaks (none found)
  • Establish radio contact with crew inside

GET 142:59:00 - Hatch Opens (4.3 minutes)

Swimmers open the Command Module hatch. First look at the crew:

What the Recovery Team Saw:

  • Three pale, haggard astronauts
  • Bearded (6 days without shaving)
  • Exhausted, barely able to move
  • Fred Haise shivering despite fever
  • But SMILING

Recovery swimmer: "They looked like hell. But they were alive, and they were home."

GET 143:02:00 - Crew Extraction (7.3 minutes)

Astronauts helped out of capsule one by one:

  1. Jack Swigert: Able to climb out with minimal help, transferred to rescue basket
  2. Jim Lovell: Climbed out, paused to look back at Odyssey one last time
  3. Fred Haise: Had to be helped out—fever, dehydration, kidney infection making him weak

GET 143:05:00 - Helicopter Lift (10.3 minutes)

Recovery helicopter lifted crew in rescue baskets:

  • 🚁 One at a time, winched up to helicopter
  • 🚁 Medical personnel in helicopter provided immediate assessment
  • 🚁 Flight to USS Iwo Jima: 2 minutes

GET 143:07:00 - On Deck (12.3 minutes)

Helicopter landed on Iwo Jima's deck. Crew walked off the helicopter under their own power.

Aboard the USS Iwo Jima

The Welcome:

600+ sailors and Marines lined the deck, cheering wildly as the astronauts stepped off the helicopter.

  • 📯 Ship's band playing
  • 🎉 Sailors waving American flags
  • 📸 Photographers capturing every moment
  • 👨‍✈️ Ship's captain waiting to greet them
  • 🎊 Celebration like the ship had never seen

Jim Lovell's First Words on Deck:

"It's great to be back. We had a few problems up there, but it's great to be back here on Earth."

(Understatement of the century!)

Immediate Medical Care:

Ship's medical team took over:

  • 🏥 Fred Haise: Rushed to sick bay immediately—IV fluids, antibiotics for kidney infection, fever treatment
  • 🏥 Jim Lovell: IV fluids for severe dehydration, vital signs monitored
  • 🏥 Jack Swigert: IV fluids, rest, observation

First Meal:

After 6 days of limited food and water:

  • 🥩 Steak
  • 🥔 Potatoes
  • 💧 Gallons of water (doctors had to limit intake—too much too fast is dangerous)
  • 🍦 Ice cream (crew's request)

Lovell: "That was the best steak I ever had. Or maybe I was just that hungry."

First Sleep:

For the first time in 6 days, the crew slept in real beds. In warm rooms. Without alarms. Without fear.

  • 😴 Lovell: Slept 12 hours straight
  • 😴 Swigert: Slept 14 hours
  • 😴 Haise: Sedated for medical treatment, slept 16+ hours

Recovery of Odyssey

Capsule Recovery:

After crew extraction, swimmers prepared Odyssey for recovery:

  • 🌊 Attached lift sling to capsule
  • 🚁 Heavy-lift helicopter hoisted capsule from water
  • 🚁 Flew capsule to Iwo Jima
  • ⚓ Lowered onto ship's deck
  • 🔒 Secured for transport back to the US

Post-Flight Inspection:

Engineers examined Odyssey immediately:

  • 🛡️ Heat shield: Charred perfectly—exactly as designed, NO DAMAGE from explosion
  • 💧 Interior: Frost on walls, condensation everywhere, but systems intact
  • 🖥️ Computer: Still running when recovered
  • 🔋 Batteries: 3.2 amp-hours remaining (barely made it!)
  • 📦 Parachutes: All three recovered intact, no cold damage
  • 📷 Film: All photo film and data recorders intact

Engineering Assessment:

"Everything that needed to work, worked. Odyssey performed flawlessly under conditions far beyond its design specifications. Remarkable."

The Journey Home

USS Iwo Jima to Pago Pago, Samoa:

  • 🚢 Travel time: 2 days at sea
  • 🏥 Medical care: Continuous monitoring and treatment
  • 💧 Rehydration: IV fluids for all three crew members
  • 😴 Rest: First real sleep in a week
  • 📞 Family calls: Crew able to call wives and children via ship's radio

Pago Pago to Houston:

  • ✈️ C-141 transport plane: NASA medical team aboard
  • ✈️ Flight time: 8 hours
  • ✈️ VIP treatment: Crew resting, recovering

April 18, 1970 - Houston, Texas:

The crew arrived at Ellington Air Force Base near Houston to a hero's welcome:

  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Families reunited: Wives, children, parents waiting
  • 🎉 Crowd of thousands: NASA personnel, media, well-wishers
  • 🎺 Military band playing
  • 📸 Global media coverage
  • 😭 Tears and hugs

Medical Recovery

Post-Flight Medical Assessment:

Fred Haise (Most Severely Affected):

  • 🏥 Hospitalized: 3 days for kidney infection treatment
  • 🤒 Fever: Took 48 hours to break
  • 💧 Dehydration: Required 2 days of IV fluids
  • ⏱️ Full recovery: 2 weeks

Jim Lovell:

  • 💧 Weight loss: 14 pounds (recovered in 10 days)
  • 💪 Muscle atrophy: Mild, from zero-G and inactivity
  • 😴 Sleep debt: Took a week to feel normal
  • ⏱️ Full recovery: 10 days

Jack Swigert:

  • 💧 Weight loss: 12 pounds (recovered in 8 days)
  • 😴 Exhaustion: Slept 12-14 hours/day for first week
  • ⏱️ Full recovery: 8 days

Final Medical Verdict:

"All three astronauts fully recovered with no permanent effects. Considering what they endured, their resilience is remarkable."

"When the helicopter landed on the Iwo's deck and I stepped out, I looked up at the blue sky and felt the warm Pacific breeze. I was home. We'd made it. Against all odds, we'd made it home." — Jim Lovell
