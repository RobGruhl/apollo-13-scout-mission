üë®‚ÄçüöÄ Command Module Pilot: Jack Swigert

Age: 38 | Background: Civilian test pilot | Experience: First spaceflight (rookie)

The Late Addition:

  • Backup crew member called up at the last minute
  • Replaced Ken Mattingly 72 hours before launch
  • Mattingly exposed to measles (ironically, never got sick)
  • Swigert's response: "I'm ready to go"

Apollo 13 Role:

  • Command Module systems expert
  • Backup pilot for spacecraft operations
  • Systems troubleshooting specialist
  • Would have orbited Moon while others landed
The Moment: Swigert flipped the switch to stir the oxygen tanks when the explosion occurred. He felt guilty but was not at fault - it was a routine procedure that had been done dozens of times before. He stayed focused despite the stress.

Fun Facts:

  • Only unmarried Apollo astronaut
  • Earned law degree while training
  • Known for great sense of humor under pressure

üë®‚ÄçüöÄ Lunar Module Pilot: Fred Haise

Age: 36 | Background: U.S. Marine Corps test pilot | Experience: First spaceflight (rookie)

The Dream:

  • Would have been the 6th person to walk on the Moon
  • Trained extensively for Fra Mauro landing
  • Lunar Module expert - knew every system intimately
  • Ended up living in the LM instead of landing in it

Apollo 13 Role:

  • Lunar Module systems expert
  • Would have piloted Moon landing (never happened)
  • Critical for LM-as-lifeboat operations
  • Engineering troubleshooting specialist
The Suffering: Haise developed a severe kidney/urinary infection during the mission due to the cold, cramped conditions and limited water for drinking. He was in significant pain but continued working. His condition worsened after splashdown and required hospitalization.

After Apollo 13:

  • Never flew to the Moon again
  • Commanded Space Shuttle approach and landing tests
  • His LM expertise saved the mission
