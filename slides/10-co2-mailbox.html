NASA's Decision

‚úì BUILD THE MAILBOX

Mission Control engineers worked frantically to design a solution using only materials aboard the spacecraft. They gathered identical items on Earth and prototyped the adapter, then radioed instructions to the crew.

The Build:

  • Square CM lithium hydroxide canisters (the actual CO2 scrubbers)
  • Plastic bags (to create air channels)
  • Cardboard from flight manual covers (structural support)
  • Duct tape (LOTS of it‚Äîto seal everything)
  • Hoses (to direct airflow)

The crew followed the instructions precisely, building the improvised adapter in zero gravity. They taped it together, connected the hoses, and held their breath...

Result: SUCCESS!

CO2 dropped from 14.9 mmHg (deadly) to 4.6 mmHg (safe)

The adapter worked perfectly. The crew could breathe again.
