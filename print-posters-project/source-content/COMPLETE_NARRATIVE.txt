=== THE SIMPLE ANSWER ===

The real answer: Three mistakes over two years.

=== MISTAKE #1: THE DROP (1968) ===
During Manufacturing:

What Happened:
‚Ä¢ Oxygen Tank #2 was dropped about 2 inches
‚Ä¢ Happened during removal before it was installed in Apollo 13's Service Module
‚Ä¢ This likely damaged its fill/drain line inside
‚Ä¢ Workers inspected the outside: looked fine
‚Ä¢ Internal damage NOT detected

The Problem:
‚Ä¢ Fill/drain system probably bent or cracked
‚Ä¢ Would cause trouble later during testing
‚Ä¢ But no one knew yet

Result: Hidden damage went unnoticed

=== MISTAKE #2: THE DESIGN CHANGE (1965) ===
The Voltage Mismatch:

Original Design:
‚Ä¢ All spacecraft systems ran on 28 volts
‚Ä¢ Thermostatic switches designed for 28 volts
‚Ä¢ Everything matched and worked safely

NASA's Change:
‚Ä¢ Upgraded ground test equipment to 65 volts
‚Ä¢ More powerful, better for testing
‚Ä¢ MOST components upgraded to handle 65 volts
‚Ä¢ BUT: Tiny thermostat switches NOT upgraded (still rated for 28V)

Why It Mattered:
‚Ä¢ Higher voltage = more heat in switches
‚Ä¢ Switches designed for 28V now carrying 65V
‚Ä¢ If they got too hot, they could weld shut
‚Ä¢ Then they couldn't turn off the heaters!

Result: Safety system compromised

=== MISTAKE #3: THE TESTING INCIDENT (March 1970) ===
Pre-Launch Ground Test (CDDT):

The Test:
‚Ä¢ March 1970 - About one month before launch
‚Ä¢ Fill oxygen tank for countdown test
‚Ä¢ Time to drain tank after test is done
‚Ä¢ Normal drain valve wouldn't work (damaged from drop?)

The "Solution":
‚Ä¢ Workers couldn't drain tank normally
‚Ä¢ So they used heaters and fans to boil off the oxygen
‚Ä¢ Ran ground power at ~65 volts
‚Ä¢ Left heaters running for about 8 HOURS to empty tank

What Actually Happened:
‚Ä¢ Temperature inside tank reached ~1,000¬∞F (540¬∞C!)
‚Ä¢ That's hot enough to melt aluminum
‚Ä¢ But temperature gauge only read up to 85¬∞F
‚Ä¢ Workers saw 85¬∞F and thought everything was fine
‚Ä¢ Real temperature was 12√ó hotter than gauge could show!

The Damage:
‚Ä¢ Thermostat switches welded shut from extreme heat
‚Ä¢ Teflon wire insulation melted and burned off
‚Ä¢ Bare electrical wires exposed inside tank
‚Ä¢ Pure oxygen environment (extremely flammable)
‚Ä¢ Bomb waiting to explode

Warning Signs Missed:
‚Ä¢ Unusual readings during test
‚Ä¢ But technicians didn't understand what they meant
‚Ä¢ Tank passed pressure tests (pressure was fine)
‚Ä¢ Exterior looked normal
‚Ä¢ Cleared for flight
